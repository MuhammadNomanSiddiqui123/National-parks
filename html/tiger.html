Malayan tiger

The Malayan tiger, scientifically known as *Panthera tigris jacksoni*, is a subspecies native to the southern Malay Peninsula. Characterized by its striking coat of dark stripes against a golden background, this tiger is slightly smaller than other subspecies like the Bengal tiger, with a more slender build. It primarily inhabits tropical forests, relying on dense vegetation for hunting prey such as deer and wild boar. Unfortunately, the Malayan tiger faces significant threats from habitat destruction and poaching, leading to its classification as endangered. Caspian tiger

The Caspian tiger, a now-extinct subspecies, once inhabited the regions surrounding the Caspian Sea, including parts of modern-day Turkey, Iran, and Central Asia. Known for its robust build and adaptability to diverse environments, it thrived in various habitats, from dense forests to rugged mountains. With a distinctive coat featuring bold stripes, the Caspian tiger was larger than some of its relatives, which added to its majestic presence. Unfortunately, it faced severe threats from habitat loss, hunting, and depletion of prey species, leading to its decline. The last confirmed sightings of this subspecies occurred in the 1970s, marking the end of its existence. Siberian tiger

The Siberian tiger, also known as the Amur tiger, is the largest of all tiger subspecies, primarily found in the forests of eastern Russia, particularly in the Russian Far East, as well as parts of northeastern China. Renowned for its impressive size, with males weighing between 400 to 600 pounds, it features a thick, pale coat with fewer and lighter stripes, adaptations that help it survive in harsh, cold climates. These solitary animals are skilled hunters, primarily preying on deer and wild boar. Despite its strength and adaptability, the Siberian tiger faces significant threats from habitat loss, poaching, and reduced prey availability, leading to its classification as endangered. Conservation efforts are critical to ensuring the survival of this magnificent species and its forest habitat.
